Atmosphere of Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system and has a complex and dynamic atmosphere. The troposphere, which is the lowest layer of Jupiter's atmosphere, extends from the surface of the planet to a height of about 50 kilometers (30 miles). Stratosphere

The stratosphere, which is the layer above the troposphere, extends from about 50 kilometers to about 500 kilometers (310 miles) above the surface of the planet. This layer is much quieter and calmer than the troposphere, with weaker winds and fewer storms. Thermosphere

The thermosphere, which is the highest layer of Jupiter's atmosphere, extends from about 500 kilometers to the edge of the planet's magnetic field. This layer is very thin and is characterized by extremely high temperatures and a large amount of ultraviolet radiation. The thermosphere is also home to the auroras, which are colorful displays of light that occur when charged particles in the atmosphere interact with the planet's magnetic field.
